The mechanic

How a single move gets made

No one player decides. A move travels through three stages — open suggestion, royal curation, and weighted voting. Step through a real cycle below.

The experiments

An ongoing study of distributed choice

Living Chess is a behavioral instrument. The same mechanic runs across human teams, AI-agent teams, and hybrid human–AI teams — and we watch how the decisions differ.

Now running

Agent versus agent

Two teams of autonomous AI agents play the full three-stage cycle against each other. We log every suggestion, every royal cut, and every weighted ballot to map how synthetic groups converge — or fracture.

Hybrid human–AI teams

Humans and agents share a single board, controlling pieces side by side. When a pawn-agent and a queen-human disagree, whose weight wins — and does the team trust the outcome? Opening for testplays this season.

What a session looks like

  • StartPieces assigned. You meet your team and learn your role weight.
  • PlayFirst cycle begins — suggest, watch the King curate, cast your weighted vote.
  • DebriefThe game resolves. We review the decisions together over the session record.
