## 25th International Conference Communication & Culture — Wrocław

Living Chess returned to the **Communication & Culture Conference** in Wrocław for a second year, this time presented by **Firangiz Aslanova**, the project's CTO and data scientist.

### The presentation

Her talk focused on the team's *non-technological solution*, highlighting Living Chess as a **performative format** with potential for community integration and social-science experiments.

The presentation showed how the concept enables:

- **Collaborative, embodied interaction** — participants physically or virtually inhabiting a role within the game
- A tool for analysing **communication, group behaviour, and collective decision-making** within participatory cultural settings
- The potential for **structured observation** of how hierarchy, weight, and constraint shape outcomes

### Continuity across years

Returning to the same conference with new material — moving from Shabnam Mammadova's conceptual proposal in 2024 to Firangiz Aslanova's presentation of a working format in 2025 — marks the project's evolution from idea to instrument.

This session reinforced Living Chess's relevance to discussions on culture, storytelling, and contemporary interactive performance.
