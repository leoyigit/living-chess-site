## 24th International Conference Communication & Culture — Wrocław

For the first time, Living Chess was brought into an academic discussion at the **Communication & Culture Conference** in Wrocław.

### The presentation

**Shabnam Mammadova** presented the project as a **conceptual proposal**, showing how:

- Player personalities, mapped through **MBTI types**, interact with chess piece roles
- These roles shift during the course of the game — creating a natural mapping between personality type and behavioural constraint
- The interaction of personality and role could serve as a framework for **sociological observation** in future implementations

### What was and wasn't there yet

At the time of the presentation, the game had not yet been played. This was deliberately a *conceptual* session — presenting the framework to gather feedback from researchers and cultural practitioners before the first live sessions.

The questions the presentation raised:

- How do these dynamics influence **behaviour and decision-making** in practice, not just theory?
- What does **group interaction** look like when personality type is fixed to piece role?
- How do constraints on movement (each piece can only move in certain ways) map to constraints on social behaviour?

### What it opened

This initial academic engagement provided valuable insights that informed the project's development, marking the beginning of its integration into **sociological research contexts**.

The conference audience's responses shaped the direction of subsequent testplays — particularly around how to observe and record group dynamics without interfering with the game's natural flow.
