## CreateCulture Lab Sprint — Vilnius

The first sprint at **CreateCulture Space** in Vilnius marked a practical beginning for the Living Chess team.

### What happened

During this period, the team:

- **Built the core team** that would carry the project forward
- Outlined the **project's initial structure** — the role-based mechanic, the three-stage decision cycle, and the research instrument framing
- Made **key calculations** to guide its development: audience sizing, session logistics, resource requirements
- Received **professional mentorship** that helped provide focus and momentum

**Oksana Duma** presented the concept in detail, covering:

- The project's purpose and core mechanic
- Target users — theatres, educational institutions, chess communities, and digital nomads
- An overview of the market, USP, and roadmap

### The accelerator

The project gained access to the accelerator program during the sprint. The team ultimately decided not to join — choosing instead to develop independently with the clarity the sprint had provided.

That decision proved right. The sprint's mentorship gave us a framework; the independence gave us the freedom to take the project somewhere the framework couldn't predict.
