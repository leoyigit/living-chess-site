## KISMIF 2024 — Porto

At the seventh **KISMIF International Conference**, *"DIY Cultures, Democracy and Creative Participation"*, Living Chess was presented by **Azad Heydarov** under the title:

> *Exploring Collective Decision-Making Through the Living Chessboard*

### The presentation

The focus was on the intersection of a collaborative, team-based experience combining strategic gameplay with devised theatre and digital storytelling.

The presentation outlined:

- The project's approach to **collective decision-making** through role-based constraints
- **Participatory storytelling** — how narrating the game in real time changes player behaviour
- **Emerging forms of cultural production** that sit between game design, theatre, and research
- The development pathway through sprints and accelerator programs
- Potential for engaging audiences and fostering dialogue within DIY and digital creative communities

### Why KISMIF

KISMIF (Keep It Simple, Make It Fantastic) gathers researchers studying participatory cultures, underground scenes, and grassroots creative practices. Living Chess fits naturally — it is a format that emerges from the bottom up, where every participant has agency, and where the rules themselves generate the narrative.
